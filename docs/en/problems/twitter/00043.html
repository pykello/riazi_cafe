Problem 43. Coloring the Map of Iran

-

This is one of the most important problems in the history of graph theory:

What is the minimum number of colors needed to color the provinces of Iran so that no two neighboring provinces have the same color?

Additional problems:

  • What is the size of the largest subset of Iranian provinces that can be colored with 3 colors so that no two neighboring provinces have the same color?

  • Question 1, but what about 2 colors?

  • Question 1, but what about 1 color?

(link to the map: https://skilledup.ir/iran-map/)

Link to the problem on Twitter: https://twitter.com/Riazi_Cafe/status/1707280079318098026

-

The minimum number of colors for coloring the provinces of Iran is 4. Answers to additional questions: 28 provinces can be colored with 3 colors. 21 provinces can be colored with 2 colors. 12 provinces can be colored with one color.

The provinces cannot be colored with 3 colors because of the following argument: Some provinces (such as Isfahan) have an odd number of neighbors that are adjacent to each other like a chain, and to paint these neighbors, at least 3 colors are required, and the color of the middle province must be different from these 3 colors.
